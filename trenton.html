Welcome to Trenton!

Right along the banks of the pretty Delaware River, Trenton, New Jersey, stands as the state capital and a city rich in history and cultural significance. Involved in revolutionary heritage, Trenton played an important role in the American Revolution, hosting the iconic Battle of Trenton in 1776. Today, its historic charm is shown in landmarks like the Old Barracks Museum and the New Jersey State House, where important decisions that shaped the nation were made. Art and culture thrive in Trenton's diverse neighborhoods, with galleries and theaters. With a new waterfront, fun parks, and an up and cming culinary scene, Trenton continues to evolve while honoring its past, making it a great destination for both residents and visitors.

Fun things to do in Trenton

Trenton, New Jersey, has a ton of activities for visitors looking for fun and exploration. Start your journey by immersing yourself in history at the Old Barracks Museum, a well-preserved testament to Trenton's Revolutionary War. Take a peaceful stroll through the beautiful Cadwalader Park, a park that has walking paths, gardens, and a charming gazebo. Art lovers can experience contemporary works at the Artworks Trenton gallery, while the vibrant Trenton Farmers Market provides a high quality food with fresh local produce and artisanal treats. For some excitement, catch a performance at the historic Patriots Theater or try your luck at the nearby Sun National Bank Center. With its blend of history, culture, and entertainment, Trenton invites you to enjoy a day filled with diverse and enjoyable experiences.
